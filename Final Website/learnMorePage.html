What are the top 5 causes of road accidents?

Top causes for Accidents in Australia:

1. Speeding: Speeding accounts for one of the major causes of accidents in Australia.
2. Distraction: navigation, cell phones, and sound, which increases the number of accidents caused by people being distracted.
3. Drive after drunk: Approximately 23% of the total accidents were alcohol related.
4. Fatigue: Tired driving will hinder the driver's reaction ability and decision-making level, increasing the probability of accidents.
5. Young Drivers: Drivers who are not eligible account for the majority of accidents.

When do road accidents happen frequently? Day or night?

Perhaps to many people's surprise, there are far more traffic accidents during the day than at night. Statistics show that more than half of accidents occur during the day, and only one-third of accidents happen at night.

Which region has a higher fatality rate in traffic accidents?

To answer this question, you need to understand that the Metropolitan Southeast and Metropolitan Northeast deaths were 27% and 19%, respectively, accounting for half of the total! On the other hand, the death rate in the Western region is 9.8 percent, which means the accident rate is not high.

What road accident type always causes more life loss?

Oh, of course, the worst kind of accident is a vehicle-to-vehicle collision. It has a high fatality rate. Conversely, events such as falling from a moving vehicle are less harmful.

Which region has the highest incidence of driving into animals?

The region with the highest animal incidence is the Northern Region of Victoria, with 22%. The North Eastern Region comes second with 16% incidence with animals.

Safe driving tips for Victoria

Allow Extra Time for your trip

Your journey may take longer than you expect. Some of Victoria’s roads have varying conditions and can be narrow and winding. Make sure your plan includes rest stops.

Watch out for animal hazards

Look out for animals strayed into the road, especially on nearby country roads, while you are enjoying the beautiful scenery of sunset and sunrise.

Driving without taking pictures, taking pictures without driving

Keep your eyes on the road, not the scenery. If you want to look at the view, make sure you pull completely off the road in a safe place.

Seasonal Road Closures

Many roads and tracks are seasonally closed to vehicle traffic each year.

Check the Weather Forecast

Weather conditions can change quickly. Always bring protective clothing in case the weather changes.

Be Bushfire Aware

Follow these bushfire safety tips to ensure that your experience is safe and enjoyable. Familiarise yourself with important bushfire safety information.

Watch out for extreme weather

Many tourists stay here at night to watch the penguins, wary of extreme weather, such as an extreme cold.

Natural Spaces

When driving, pay attention not to go on illegal roads and protect the ecological environment.

Drive within the limits & the conditions

There are different speeds limit near the scenic spots. Stay within the speed limit and always choose an appropriate speed for the driving conditions.

Share roads with tram

When a tram comes to a halt and tram doors open, cars must stop immediately, and give way to passengers stepping on or getting off the tram.

Look out the Hook turns

The hook turn is a road rule specific to Melbourne. To ensure that trams get a clear run through some intersections, drivers turning right must do so from the left-hand lane.

General road rules

--Cars travel on the left of the road.

--The speed is in kilometers (km) per hour, not miles per hour.

--Seat belts are compulsory in front and back seats.

--Drivers are required to carry a license at all times.

--Blood alcohol limit of 0.05, Random breath tests operate around the state.
